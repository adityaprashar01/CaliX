# Safety & Child Protection

Calix is designed for children and their parents. Safety is not an add-on feature. It is a core product requirement.

## Product safety principles

Calix is built around two rules:

1. **Kids should experience movement as fun, structured, and motivating**
2. **Parents should experience the product as safe, controlled, and evidence-backed**

## What Calix does

### 1. Parent-linked child usage
Children are not treated as fully independent users.
Parent consent and parent-child linking are part of the intended product flow.

### 2. Role separation
Calix separates child, parent, and school/teacher journeys so each audience sees the right experience and level of control.

### 3. No open messaging
Calix does **not** support open chat between kids.

### 4. Limited social interactions
Any social or challenge-based interaction is intended to be:
- positive-only
- limited in scope
- non-open-ended
- designed to reduce misuse risk

### 5. Safety-reviewed workout design
Workout content is intended to be:
- short
- age-appropriate
- no-equipment by default
- reviewed for safety before release

Calix should not generate or publish unsafe workout content.

### 6. Parent visibility
Parents should be able to see:
- activity streaks
- completed quests
- weekly summaries
- progress indicators
- safety labels where relevant

### 7. Minimal-risk AI usage
AI in Calix is intended for:
- encouragement
- simple summaries
- next-step suggestions

AI is **not** intended to:
- diagnose injuries
- provide medical advice
- replace expert supervision
- generate unsafe or unreviewed workout plans

All AI output should be:
- short
- positive
- age-appropriate
- non-medical
- tightly scoped

## Privacy principles

Because Calix involves child users, privacy must be treated seriously.

### We aim to follow these principles:
- collect the minimum data needed for the product to work
- separate parent and child roles clearly
- protect minor data with strict access controls
- avoid exposing sensitive child information broadly
- keep school reporting aggregated where possible
- design flows to align with child privacy expectations, including India DPDP-style requirements

## What Calix does not do
- no open direct messaging between kids
- no unsafe public social feed by default
- no AI-generated workouts without safety review
- no unnecessary exposure of sensitive child data
- no medical claims or medical treatment promises

## Important note

Calix is a habit-building fitness product, not a medical device or clinical service.

Parents and caregivers should use judgment and seek professional advice where needed, especially for children with injuries, pain, medical conditions, or movement restrictions.
